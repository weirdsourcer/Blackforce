Mtchew! for a start, that gbẹ̀gìrì isn't even it,
too light!
Shows that the person that cooked it didn't boil
out the essense that formed the flavour of the soup.

Don't let me start on the ewéédú sha,
everything hints at it being overcooked,
yet stale,
it is simply too thick to pass the grade in the
culinary school of ìbílẹ̀ delicacy that's worth it's star.

And is that really àmàlà you're presenting to me
or an ẹbọ to the Òrìṣàs?
What was used to sieve the èlùbọ́ that went into the pot that
produced this abomination of a what-can-we-even-name-this?
A properly trained àmàlà stirer with half an eye would find
a million kókó in this thing!

This won't even pass the first test of swallow succulency
not to talk of the third test of àmàlà àti àbùlà gyration test.
